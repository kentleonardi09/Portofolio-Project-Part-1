Question 1: Discover Security Concepts Used on Campus

Explanation

a. Confidentiality – Access Card System at UWA  
At the University of Western Australia, many buildings and study spaces require students to scan their university access card to enter, especially after normal operating hours. This ensures that only authorised students and staff can access certain facilities. This reflects confidentiality because it prevents unauthorised individuals from entering restricted areas, similar to how login credentials protect sensitive systems and information in cybersecurity.

b. Integrity – Student Grading System (LMS)  
The Learning Management System (LMS) is used by students to submit assignments, access course materials, and view assessment marks and final grades. Once grades are finalised, only authorised lecturers or administrative staff can make changes. This demonstrates integrity because it ensures that academic records remain accurate and cannot be altered by unauthorised users, similar to how data protection mechanisms maintain correctness in cybersecurity systems.

c. Availability – Student Connect Platform  
Student Connect is an online platform used by students to enrol in units, check timetables, and manage academic services. It is especially important that the system remains accessible during peak periods such as enrolment week when many students are logging in at the same time. This reflects availability because the system must remain reliable and accessible whenever students need it, similar to how cybersecurity systems must ensure continuous service access.
